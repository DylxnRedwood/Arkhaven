# Languages of Lazuril

Numerous languages and regional dialects are spoken throughout [Lazuril](../Locations/World/Lazuril.md).

Language distribution is influenced by geography, migration, trade, religion, conquest, and historical isolation.

No single language is universally spoken across the world.

## Overview

Most major regions possess at least one dominant trade or administrative language alongside local dialects.

Multilingualism is common among:

- Merchants
- Scholars
- Sailors
- Diplomats
- Scriptors
- Clergy
- Travelling guild members

Isolated regions may preserve older linguistic forms no longer used elsewhere.

## Elven Languages

Elven languages are generally highly formalised and historically layered.

Older dialects preserve grammatical structures and vocabulary no longer found in younger languages.

Written elven records are regarded as among the most reliable historical archives in Arkhaven.

## Dwarven Languages

Dwarven languages are strongly associated with engineering, trade, contracts, mining terminology, and lineage recording.

Several dialects contain technical vocabulary not easily translated into surface languages.

Inscribed stone records remain an important part of dwarven cultural tradition.

## Human Languages

Human populations speak a wide variety of regional languages and trade dialects.

Political fragmentation and historical migration have produced substantial linguistic variation across the continent.

Several major trade languages are widely understood in urban centres.

## Religious Languages

Certain churches maintain ceremonial or scriptural languages used primarily for ritual, archive preservation, or doctrine.

These languages are often studied by Scriptors and religious scholars.

## Current Status

Languages throughout Lazuril continue to evolve through migration, trade, war, scholarship, and institutional influence.

The expansion of archive systems and long-distance trade has increased linguistic standardisation in some regions, while remote territories continue to preserve highly localised dialects.
