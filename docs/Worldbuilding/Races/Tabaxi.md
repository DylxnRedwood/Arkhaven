# Tabaxi

Tabaxi are a feline humanoid people found primarily in the northern territories of [Arkhaven](../Locations/Continents/Arkhaven.md), especially within the tropical rainforest, dense jungle, river basin, and coastal forest regions.

They are widely known for their agility, curiosity, oral traditions, sharp senses, and ability to move through difficult forest terrain with unusual speed and confidence.

In Lazuril, tabaxi culture is strongly shaped by the northern jungle environment, treetop settlement, trade secrecy, sign language, and a long history of avoiding capture by outside hunters and slavers.

## Overview

Tabaxi are an intelligent and highly mobile people with feline features, including fur, claws, tails, reflective eyes, and strong climbing ability.

They are not a single unified nation.

Most tabaxi communities are organised into kin-groups, travelling bands, treetop settlements, river clans, shrine families, and loose regional confederations.

Tabaxi culture values:

- Freedom of movement
- Curiosity
- Personal reputation
- Family protection
- Storytelling
- Skilled hunting
- Silent travel
- Trade knowledge
- Memory of routes
- Resistance to captivity

Their societies tend to be flexible rather than centralised.

## Geographic Distribution

The largest tabaxi populations live in the northern jungles and rainforests of Arkhaven.

These regions are hot, humid, densely vegetated, and difficult for outsiders to navigate.

Tabaxi settlements are most commonly found in:

- High canopy regions
- River valleys
- Rainforest interiors
- Coastal jungle belts
- Hidden ravines
- Giant tree clusters
- Forested cliffs
- Wetland margins

Smaller tabaxi communities also exist near trade routes, port settlements, and frontier towns, though many avoid permanent residence in heavily populated human regions.

## Treetop Settlements

Most established tabaxi homes are built within the upper canopy.

These settlements may include:

- Rope bridges
- Hidden platforms
- Suspended huts
- Woven walkways
- Tree-hollow stores
- Rain collectors
- Climbing routes
- Lookout perches
- Concealed nursery spaces

Treetop construction provides protection from ground predators, flooding, raiders, and hunters.

The highest and most concealed areas of a settlement are usually reserved for children, elders, food stores, and signal keepers.

Outsiders rarely see a complete tabaxi settlement.

Visitors are often received on lower platforms or forest-floor meeting grounds rather than being allowed into the upper dwellings.

## Predators and Captivity

Tabaxi communities have a long history of defending themselves against predators, raiders, and those who attempt to capture young tabaxi for sale.

This has made settlement secrecy a cultural necessity.

Tabaxi children are carefully protected, and most communities maintain strict rules regarding:

- Outsider access
- Camp locations
- Travel routes
- Night movement
- Firelight use
- Trade negotiation
- Stranger contact
- Child movement outside the canopy

Slave-taking is treated as one of the most serious crimes by tabaxi communities.

Families and neighbouring clans may cooperate across large distances to recover captured children or punish known traffickers.

## Common Sign Language

Tabaxi in Arkhaven commonly learn Common Sign Language.

This practice developed for several reasons:

- Silent hunting
- Communication across dense jungle
- Avoiding predators
- Coordination during travel
- Trade with outsiders
- Communication when speech would reveal position
- Protection during raids or pursuit

Most tabaxi children learn signs before they are considered safe to travel beyond the immediate settlement.

Sign language is also important during inter-clan meetings, negotiations, and watchkeeping.

Many tabaxi can communicate across reasonable distance using subtle hand, tail, ear, and body movements combined with formal sign.

## Language and Storytelling

Tabaxi language traditions are strongly oral.

Stories are used to preserve:

- Routes
- Warnings
- Clan memory
- Hunting knowledge
- Moral lessons
- Trade history
- Family lineages
- Encounters with outsiders

Tabaxi often treat stories as practical tools rather than entertainment alone.

A story may encode the location of a safe crossing, the habits of a dangerous beast, or the identity of a trader who cannot be trusted.

Written records exist, but they are less central than memory, song, rhythm, gesture, and repeated performance.

## Curiosity and Travel

Tabaxi are widely associated with curiosity.

This trait is often misunderstood by outsiders as restlessness or lack of seriousness.

Among tabaxi, curiosity is considered a survival skill.

A person who notices unusual tracks, strange smells, altered bird calls, broken vines, or unfamiliar trade goods may prevent disaster.

Many tabaxi leave their home regions for a time to gather stories, maps, objects, debts, songs, and news.

Some return home after years of travel.

Others become permanent wanderers, guides, merchants, scouts, performers, or adventurers.

## Trade

Tabaxi trade exists, but it is cautious and selective.

Common trade goods include:

- Rare fruits
- Medicinal plants
- Dyes
- Feathers
- Carved wood
- Jungle resins
- Spices
- Animal products
- Route knowledge
- Fine woven goods

Tabaxi are often reluctant to sell detailed maps of their homeland.

They may trade guidance, but rarely surrender full knowledge of hidden paths, settlement locations, or canopy routes.

## Religion and Belief

Tabaxi religious practice varies between communities.

Common religious themes include:

- Freedom
- Fortune
- Moonlight
- Hunting
- Stories
- Ancestral memory
- Forest spirits
- Protection of children
- Respect for dangerous places

Many tabaxi communities maintain small shrines in tree hollows, cliff shelters, or concealed groves.

Rituals are often brief, mobile, and woven into daily life rather than centred on large formal temples.

## Relationship with Other Peoples

Tabaxi relations with other peoples are shaped by caution.

They are not hostile by default, but most communities judge outsiders by action rather than stated intent.

Positive relationships are most common with:

- Honest traders
- River guides
- Healers
- Scholars who respect boundaries
- Anti-slavery groups
- Forest communities

Relations are poor with groups known for hunting, trafficking, forced labour, or exploitative trade.

Some human and coastal settlements have long-standing agreements with nearby tabaxi clans.

Other regions are avoided entirely.

## Warfare and Defence

Tabaxi warfare is usually defensive, mobile, and terrain-focused.

Common tactics include:

- Ambush
- Night movement
- Canopy travel
- Silent signalling
- Trap use
- Hit-and-run attacks
- Misdirection
- Evacuation routes
- Rescue raids

Tabaxi rarely favour direct battlefield confrontation unless forced.

Their greatest military advantage is their ability to move through jungle terrain faster and more quietly than most enemies.

Settlements are designed with layered escape routes rather than only fixed defences.

## Reputation in Arkhaven

Tabaxi have a mixed reputation across Arkhaven.

They are often described as:

- Curious
- Agile
- Secretive
- Difficult to track
- Skilled climbers
- Excellent scouts
- Unpredictable traders
- Protective of children
- Distrustful of authority

Some of these views are accurate.

Others are shaped by outsiders who do not understand tabaxi caution or who resent being denied access to northern forest routes.

## Current Status

Tabaxi communities remain strongest in the northern jungles and rainforests.

Their settlements are rarely marked accurately on outside maps.

Their people continue to play an important role as guides, scouts, traders, storytellers, messengers, and defenders of northern forest routes.

Although some tabaxi now live in cities or travel widely across Lazuril, most remain culturally tied to the canopy settlements and hidden communities of the northern territories.

Their central political concern remains unchanged: survival without captivity, trade without exposure, and freedom without surrendering the safety of their young.
