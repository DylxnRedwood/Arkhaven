# Veinstone

**Veinstone** is a rare mineral substance found in isolated deposits throughout [Lazuril](../Locations/World/Lazuril.md).

The material is most strongly associated with regions surrounding [The Godscar](../Lore/Historical%20Events/The%20Godscar.md), where exposed mineral veins have been identified within fractured cliffs, deep rock formations, and certain lake systems.

## Overview

Veinstone is typically found in narrow mineral veins embedded deep within stone.

The material varies considerably in:

- Colour
- Density
- Stability
- Luminosity
- Purity
- Structural composition

Most deposits are small and difficult to extract safely.

Large exposed veins are extremely uncommon.

## Distribution

The largest known concentrations of Veinstone are located near the Godscar.

Smaller deposits have occasionally been identified:

- Beneath mountain ranges
- Within deep excavation sites
- Near ancient ruins
- Along unstable fault systems
- Within submerged cavern structures

Several dwarven excavations have reportedly encountered Veinstone deposits deep beneath the continent.

## The Red Waters

Certain lakes and waterways near the Godscar display unusual red coloration believed to result from mineral contamination linked to Veinstone-rich regions.

The intensity of this coloration varies between locations and seasons.

Water taken from these regions is generally considered unsafe for long-term consumption.

Reported effects associated with prolonged exposure include:

- Hallucinations
- Fever
- Disorientation
- Violent dreams
- Memory irregularities
- Physical sickness

Reliable medical understanding remains limited.

## Historical Use

Historical records suggest Veinstone has occasionally been used in:

- Magical artefacts
- Ritual construction
- Religious objects
- Archive preservation
- Ancient engineering systems

Most surviving knowledge regarding its practical use is incomplete or heavily restricted.

Several institutions actively regulate access to refined Veinstone materials.

## Extraction and Regulation

Because of its rarity and uncertain properties, Veinstone extraction is heavily controlled in many regions.

Ownership disputes surrounding deposits are common between:

- Kingdoms
- Churches
- Mining guilds
- Archive Houses
- Dwarven holds

Illegal trade in Veinstone fragments and powdered derivatives is known to exist.

## Current Status

Veinstone remains poorly understood.

Although scholars, Scriptors, miners, and artificers continue to study the material, no universally accepted explanation exists regarding its origin or full properties.

Several major institutions maintain sealed archives relating to Veinstone research and historical incidents connected to its use.
