# Ancient Ruins

Ancient ruins are found throughout [Lazuril](../../Locations/World/Lazuril.md).

These structures vary considerably in age, origin, condition, and historical reliability.

Many ruins predate surviving written record.

## Overview

Ruins may include:

- Abandoned cities
- Buried temples
- Fortress remains
- Tunnel systems
- Observatory complexes
- Flooded settlements
- Collapsed roads
- Tomb structures
- Archive chambers
- Unknown subterranean halls

Some ruins are partially inhabited or repurposed by later populations.

## Historical Importance

Ancient ruins are major sources of:

- Historical evidence
- Relics
- Magical artefacts
- Lost records
- Religious claims
- Political legitimacy

Several churches and kingdoms base territorial or doctrinal claims upon ruins connected to older traditions.

## Exploration

Ruins attract:

- Scholars
- Relic hunters
- Adventurers
- Scriptors
- Treasure seekers
- Cults
- Archaeological expeditions

Exploration is often dangerous.

## Hazards

Common dangers associated with ruins include:

- Structural collapse
- Magical instability
- Hidden chambers
- Environmental hazards
- Ancient traps
- Hostile creatures
- Religious conflict
- Unstable excavation

Some sites are intentionally sealed or restricted.

## Major Categories

Scholars commonly classify ruins into broad categories.

These include:

- Pre-Fracture ruins
- Godscar ruins
- Pre-Silence settlements
- Abandoned dwarven excavations
- Lost pilgrimage sites
- Buried coastal structures
- Unknown deep-earth ruins

## Current Status

Ancient ruins remain central to historical scholarship and exploration throughout Lazuril.

Many major discoveries continue to reshape understanding of earlier historical ages.

At the same time, several institutions restrict access to ruins considered politically, religiously, or magically dangerous.
